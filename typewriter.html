TypeWritter

A typewriter is a mechanical or electromechanical device used for writing text on paper. It was commonly used before the advent of computers and word processors. Typewriters feature a keyboard with keys corresponding to characters, numbers, and symbols. When a key is pressed, it causes a typebar or a print head to strike an inked ribbon against the paper, transferring the character onto the page. Early typewriters were purely mechanical, while later models incorporated electric motors for power. Typewriters revolutionized writing and office work, offering a faster and more efficient way to produce documents compared to handwriting. Despite being largely replaced by computers, typewriters remain popular among collectors and enthusiasts for their nostalgia and unique mechanical charm.
