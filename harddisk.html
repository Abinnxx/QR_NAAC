Hard Disk

A hard disk, commonly known as a hard drive, is a primary storage device used in computers for long-term data storage. It consists of one or more rigid disks coated with a magnetic material and housed within a sealed unit. Hard disks are typically installed inside the computer and provide much larger storage capacities compared to floppy disks and other removable storage media. They are commonly available in sizes ranging from several hundred gigabytes (GB) to multiple terabytes (TB), offering ample space for storing operating systems, software applications, documents, multimedia files, and more. Hard disks use spinning platters and read/write heads to access and store data magnetically. Despite competition from solid-state drives (SSDs), which offer faster data access speeds and are more durable due to their lack of moving parts, hard disks remain a popular and cost-effective storage solution for many computing needs.
