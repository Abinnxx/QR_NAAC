Floppy Disk

A floppy disk, often referred to simply as a floppy, was a common form of portable storage in the late 20th century. It was a thin, flexible disk encased in a plastic shell, usually 3.5 inches wide. Floppy disks had limited storage capacities, typically around 1.44 MB, making them suitable for storing small files and documents. They were widely used for transferring data between computers, storing software installations, and backing up important files. However, with the advent of higher-capacity and more reliable storage solutions such as CDs, DVDs, and USB flash drives, floppy disks gradually became obsolete and are no longer in common use today.
