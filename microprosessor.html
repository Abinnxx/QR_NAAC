Micro Processor

A microprocessor, also known as a CPU, is like the brain of a computer. It's a tiny chip made of silicon that does all the thinking and calculations necessary for the computer to work. It reads and follows instructions stored in memory, performs calculations, and controls how different parts of the computer work together. Microprocessors come in different types, with varying speeds and features, but they all do the same basic job of running programs and making your computer work. They're found in everything from laptops and smartphones to appliances and cars, powering all sorts of electronic devices we use every day.
